About Me

My programming story beings in my Senior year of high school. I took AP Computer Science and learned Java. I loved the multifaceted problem solving apporach that programming offered, and before I knew it, I was hooked. I applied to college as a Computer Science major, and was admitted to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. The rest is history - I have been taking a plethora of programming courses, and have decided to pursue a dual degree in Information Technology and Web Sciences (ITWS). If you so desire, you can follow my growth and progress as a budding developer via my Github repo.

On campus, I am the Vice President of the Troy Consulting Club, a member of the Rubik's Cube Club, as well as the Computer Science Club. Fun fact: I am an avid speed-cuber, and thoroughly enjoy collecting and solving a variety of puzzles. I first participated in my school's Hackathon, HackRPI, in November 2016, and was a repeat contestant in our 2017 Hackathon. I am also a Brother in my campus' Phi Sigma Kappa Fraternity (Gamma Tetarton) chapter, and I also intend on participating in the campus open source club, RCOS (Rensselaer Center for Open Source Software) at some point in my college career. When I am not doing school work or hanging out with my friends, I can usually be found taking Tae Kwon Do classes, playing golf, or reading. I am actually a 3rd Dan black belt, and have been involved with Tae Kwon Do for 14 years.

Feel free to download my resume, and if you have any questions, you can contact me at schnod@rpi.edu. Thank you!

Rubik's Cubes

My cubing journey started when I was in 2nd grade (2006). I found my Mom's original cube from the 1980s. She never solved it, and said she wouldn't mind if I tried it. I sat down at my grandparent's computer and loaded up this "great new site" called YouTube! I searched for a tutorial and found the original "Dan Brown" cube tutorial, and tried following along. Unfortunately, at the the time I didn't have the patience to sit there and figure out what he was saying.

That all changed two years later in the 4th grade, when a sudden Rubik's Cube craze swept Joyce Kilmer Elementary School. I wanted to fit in, so I found the tutorial again. After a half hour, I finally solved the cube. From then on, I was hooked! I began timing myself to get faster, and after almost 10 years of cube solving, I have my solve times down to about 25 seconds and have amassed a collection of 100+ different cubes. There's a lot of room for improvement though!

Tae Kwon Do

I began my Tae Kwon Do career at Bai's Tae Kwon Do in Ramsey NJ 14 years ago, when I was five years old. What started as a way for me to learn self defense, if I ever needed it, has turned into a near lifelong journey that has helped me handle stress, be independent, develop self control, healthy habits, and a competitive spirit. But, most importantly are the friends I have made along the way.

In 2012, I received my 3rd Dan black belt, and in 2015, I moved to a different Tae Kwon Do school. I currently practice at DPMA in Westwood NJ, and I highly recommend them! The instructors are very friendly, have helped me perfect techniques, and have fundamentally changed what it means for me to be a black belt.
